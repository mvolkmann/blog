Setup

  1. Browse developer.apple.com.

  2. Click "Account" and sign in.

  3. Click "Certificates, Identifiers & Profiles".

  4. In the left nav, click "Identifiers".

  5. Click the "+" after the heading "Identifiers".

  6. Select the "App IDs" radio button.

  7. Click the "Continue" button.

  8. Select "App".

  9. Click the "Continue" button.

  10. Enter an app description.

  11. Paste the app bundle ID.

  12. Under "Capabilities", check the checkbox for "WeatherKit".

  13. Click the "Continue" button.

  14. In the left nav, click "Services".

  15. Under "WeatherKit", click "View".

  16. Click the "Continue" button.

  17. Click the "Register" button.

  18. Is it also necessary to create a provisioning profile here?

    1. In the left nav, click "Profiles".
    2. Click the "+" after the heading "Profiles".
    3. Select the "iOS App Development" radio button.
    4. Click the "Continue" button.
    5. Select an app ID from the dropdown.
    6. Click the "Continue" button.
    7. Select an existing certificate.
    8. Click the "Continue" button.
    9. Select the checkboxes for the devices to be included.
    10. Click the "Continue" button.
    11. Enter a profile name (could use the app name).
    12. Click the "Generate" button.
    13. Click the "Download" button.
    14. Double-click the downloaded .mobileprovision file to install the profile.

  19. In Xcode, click the top entry in the Navigator.

  20. Select the target that will use WeatherKit.

  21. Click the "Signing & Capabilities" tab.

  22. Click the "+" in the upper-left.

  23. Find WeatherKit and double-click it.

It may be necessary to wait a half hour before trying to use WeatherKit from an app. I got the errors "Mescal Failed", "Error Domain=WeatherDaemon.WDSJWTAuthenticatorService.Errors", and "Encountered an error when fetching weather data subset" when I tried immediately.
